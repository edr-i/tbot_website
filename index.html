T-Bot

Just do it. Taste the feeling.

In a nutshell.

The main goal of this project is to build a centipede-inspired robot so that we can learn more about this biological structure and use its advantages for further research in the future. The robot will use the same movement mechanics as the real-life centipede species. To achieve that, the robot will incorporate actuators to move within the environment, sensors to record the movements and axis positions, a controller and a 3D-printed frame. These all work together to create a device than can be controlled from the user in specialized scenarios and display the interesting characteristics of this fascinating creature.

That is what we're trying to do, summed up. Feel free to head over to the Product page to get detailled infos about how we're doing it, Statistics to see what our robot has achieved until now, or just keep scrolling to get a better understanding of why we're doing all this.

Where we're coming from.

In November 2019 Albania was struck by a strong earthquake, powerful enough to be felt as far as Italy and Serbia. It collapsed 2000 buildings, displaced 17000 people and the death toll of 51 made it the world's deadliest earthquake in 2019.

Albania received assistance from 29 countries around the globe. With no real infrastructure, personnel and most importantly equipment to deal with such a catastrophe, the whole country relied on international help. It was devastating to see thousands of families in desparate need of help and the state with no opportunity to solve a problem of this degree.

Our device, although only interesting for a niche, aims to bring back in a way or another to the place we were raised in. However minimal or life-changing of an impact it will have, we want to feel that we contributed to our community in the way we know best: through technology.

A bit about the centipede.

A centipede is a type of myriapod with many legs. Centipedes are predators. They hunt during the night; they are nocturnal. Some centipedes are venomous, but none can kill a person unless they are allergic. Centipedes are in the class Chilopoda, and related to millipedes, which are not poisonous. As the millipedes, they are segmented. They have between 5 and 173 segments, but only one pair of legs per segment. There may be 8,000 species in the world.

What is interesting for us about this creature is their method of movement (also known as locomotion). Centipedes display a much more different body-limb coordination compared to other animals. They walk by propagating a wave of leg movement along the length of the body, so that each leg moves in strict sequence. A demo can be seen on the right.

Advantages of centipede locomotion.

The above mentioned unique way of movement brings with it a multitude of advantages as compared to other methods of movement. The most important ones are presented below.


Agility

Such a flexible body allows centipedes to display remarkable agility when faced with obstacles. The body can comform to the surface it's on and get over objects in the way with ease.

Robustness

Studies have shown that the many-legged design enables graceful degradation, as opposed to immediate failure. This can be crucial in terrains of high danger, making the device prone to damage.

Adaptability

This build is highly adaptable to uneven terrain. In the inevitable cases that the robot is presented with difficult terrain, it can easily manage to make its way across.

Use cases for a centipede robot.

Sure, all these advantages that this multilegged movement brings are nice, but what are some real-life use cases for it? Deriving from our own experiences and through research, we have identified some meaningful implementation opportunities for our project.


Search and Rescue

As mentioned above, a robot like this is very well suited for being part of search and rescue teams after a nature catastrophe. Its ability to navigate difficult terrain can enable it to get in difficult places which people can't physically reach.

Environment monitoring

With the addition of cameras or sensors (or both!) to the robot's housing, data can be recorded while the robot is moving. This data can then be used to monitor a specific terrain and analyze different factors about it.

Transporting goods

It's clear that a pattern is starting to form here. The invaluable advantage of being able to move in difficult environments also allow the robot to be used as a mean to transport items between two places. Of course there are many already existing devices for this job, but T-Bot could be useful because of its agility and low cost to build.
